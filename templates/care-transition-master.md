# CareOS: Long-Range Caregiving Transition Manifest

This master checklist tracks critical environmental, legal, and operational adjustments across the timeline of chronic or degenerative patient care (such as Parkinson's, dementia, or severe mobility decline). 

---

## 🏡 Phase 1: Proactive Space Optimization & Home De-Cluttering
*Goal: Modify the physical environment to prevent emergency incidents (falls, breaks) before severe symptoms arrive.*

- [ ] **High-Traffic Clear-Outs:** Remove loose rugs, floor cords, low coffee tables, and structural clutter from the primary pathways (hallways, bedroom, kitchen).
- [ ] **Lighting Upgrades:** Install automatic plug-in motion-sensor nightlights along the path from the bed to the bathroom.
- [ ] **Contrast & Visibility Assessments:** Mark unexpected steps or flooring changes with high-visibility non-slip tape or contrasting thresholds.
- [ ] **Bathroom Reinforcement:** Pre-emptively install secure grab bars in the shower and next to the toilet. (Do not rely on suction-cup bars).
- [ ] **The "Keep-Safe" Sorting:** Begin sorting through old storage rooms, attics, and paperwork *with* the parent early, organizing vital personal effects vs. items to donate or downsize before cognitive or physical stamina declines.

---

## ⚖️ Phase 2: Essential Legal & Estate Infrastructure
*Goal: Secure explicit consent and management delegation while the patient has full cognitive autonomy.*

- [ ] **Durable Power of Attorney (POA):** Establish a legal financial POA designation so a trusted family member can manage accounts and pay medical bills if the parent becomes incapacitated.
- [ ] **Advance Healthcare Directive (Living Will):** Document explicit preferences for life-prolonging treatments, resuscitation (DNR orders), and end-of-life care decisions.
- [ ] **Healthcare Power of Attorney (Healthcare Proxy):** Designate who is legally authorized to make medical decisions if the patient cannot speak for themselves.
- [ ] **Asset Consolidation Mapping:** Create a localized list of all bank accounts, insurance policies, active utility bills, and safe deposit boxes (store this securely in a locked file or encrypted password vault).

---

## 🩺 Phase 3: Clinical Baseline & Logistics Setup
*Goal: Transition from reactive crisis management to structured medical tracking.*

- [ ] **The Physician Directory:** Build a centralized list of all attending doctors, specialists, physical therapists, and local emergency contacts.
- [ ] **Prescription Tracking Audit:** Log a master baseline of all active medications, precise daily schedules, dosages, and contraindications.
- [ ] **Insurance Coverage Map:** Verify Medicare, Medicaid, or long-term care insurance policy details, confirming coverage boundaries for home health aides, durable medical equipment (DBS, walkers, wheelchairs), and physical therapy sessions.
- [ ] **Digital Care Portal Initialization:** Set up and test logins for the parent's online medical chart portals (e.g., MyChart) so the care team can instantly track test results and view upcoming appointment schedules.

---

## 👥 Phase 4: Caregiver Care & Network Delegation
*Goal: Distribute the emotional and operational load to avoid solo-caregiver burnout.*

- [ ] **The "First-Aid & Chill Kit" Note:** Assemble a portable bag containing emergency medication loops, noise-canceling earplugs, simple diagnostic tracking tools, and emergency contact codes.
- [ ] **The Division of Labor Chart:** Explicitly split duties (e.g., Friend A manages calendar logistics/doctor portal; Friend B manages grocery and physical meal drops) so the main caregiver is not handling 100% of the cognitive overhead.
- [ ] **Respite Care Backup System:** Identify a secondary family member, local adult day center, or professional home health aide service who can step in for a designated weekend or day window each month to give the primary caregiver a mandatory wellness buffer.
